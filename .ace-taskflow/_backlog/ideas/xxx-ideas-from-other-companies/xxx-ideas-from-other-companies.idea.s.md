These are our biggest takeaways:

Lesson 1: Don't build a single, all-powerful agent. Instead, create a team of specialized agents that work together (It's more effective).

Lesson 2: The LLM is just the engine. The real work is building the car around it, the architecture, the memory, the tools.

Lesson 3: Agents will fail. It's inevitable. The key is to build systems that can handle errors gracefully and help the human in the loop.

Lesson 4: Start with a ridiculously narrow focus. A simple agent that masters one task is far more valuable than a complex one that's unreliable.
