Recent advancements in artificial intelligence, particularly in the field of Large Language Models (LLMs), have shown significant potential for revolutionizing various industries. This research focuses on the application of LLMs in automated code generation and documentation. Key findings indicate that models like Google Gemini and OpenAI's GPT series can effectively assist developers by generating boilerplate code, suggesting optimizations, and creating comprehensive documentation from source code or functional descriptions.

However, challenges remain in ensuring the accuracy and security of generated code, as well as managing the computational resources required for training and inference. Future work will explore hybrid approaches combining LLMs with traditional static analysis tools to enhance reliability and integrate these capabilities seamlessly into existing development workflows. The goal is to improve developer productivity while maintaining high standards of code quality and security.
